# The Spy Game

It's a race between resolving the situation, and the situation going
bad, exploding, etc. All while protection operational security, cover,
etc.

Lots of randomness and unknowns. The situation clock is counting down,
it's fallout pool is building up. You've got to make enough progress
and then resolve it before it blows up.

Maybe we can tie the fallout pool building up with the clock counting
down? The bigger the pool, (and the larger it's dice) the more likely
it is to blow up? I'm thinking that one of the things the GM can do
with a "failed die" is to roll a fallout pool. If it's top 2 dice
total over some threshold it blows up in the way described by that
total.

Situations are resolved a little like Ironsworn oaths. You put as much
progress into a situation as you can before you feel you have to pull
the trigger to resolve it. The less progress you put in the less
likely it is to be resolved to your satisfaction but the longer you
wait the more fallout accumulates and the more likely it is to blow up
before you can resolve it. Push your luck all over the place at
multiple levels.

## Moves

### Take Care of It

* Intel makes this easier
* Success leads to either progress or data
* GM sets minimum die size based on "position"
  * Controlled - 6
  * Standard - 8
  * Risky - 10
* Player may always push and roll larger dice

Player rolls 2 dice of their chosen size.

For each die that rolls below their skill (and is not the max a die
can roll) the player may choose 1
* add a die of the same size to the resolution pool
* gain 1 data

For each die that rolls at or above their skill, or is the max a die
can roll, the GM may choose 1
* Roll fallout
* add a die of the chosen size to the fallout pool
* Start a new situation

#### Rolling fallout

When the GM rolls fallout, they roll the whole pool, and total the top
two dice.

1-8:   The situation isn't as bad as it seems. the Players may remove
       one of the lowest rolls
9-12:  The situation reveals something critical
       to the players. +1 Intelligence
13-16: Some unexpected twist, complicating matters, casts doubt on 1
       piece of intelligence (-1)
17-20: Bad case
21-23: Worst case
24:    Worst possible case
