# Modular Otherkind Dice

Games in which otherkind dice is made super crunchy.

Units, champions, characters, mondsters, situations, armies, whatever
each come with dice to roll and slots to assign them to a la otherkind
dice. The slots have game effects when dice are assigned. positive
ones when high rolls are assigned, negative ones otherwise.

Defensive or reactive modules are triggered by fictional
positions. For example armor is triggered when someone wearing it is
attacked. It's effect is negative to the attacker (reduces damage)
unless a high enough die is spent on it.

Modules (like moves) describe the fictional positions that cause their
inclusion in a roll. "Whenever the wearer of the armor is attacked",
"When you fire this gun at a target"

This feels fun for Mecha battles, space combat, scifi shit.

Tons of stuff at lots of different levels could be managed this
way. The dungeon room could have a slot that triggers a trap if you
fight or rush through the room. Certain tactics (like kiting, turtling
up, coordinated lance charges, feints, Kronsky's defense.

propaganda, espionage, recon, reporting, "hearts and minds"
reinforcements.

# Otherkind Dice Hex Crawl

Different hexes could have different risks, Moving through a hex
causes a roll.

# The work

The work for this kind of game woudl be writing tons of content for
various modules. Different terrains, enhabitants, nations, cultures,
monsters, gear, powers, tactics, etc. This seems like it could start
to feel like the fun parts of 3.5/Pathfinder with possibly fewer
specific rules to learn, and more flexibility of what you might want
to inlude or exclude from your game.

don't want the weather module? no problem, the politics one? no
problem. No hex crawling for you? sure. Want the monsters but not the
tactics? cool.
